Accessible Architecture in Schools

Bangla

Education is the cornerstone for personal development and societal progress. Accessible architecture within schools is a fundamental right ensuring that every student can engage in learning and social interactions without barriers.

We delve into the significant impact of architectural design on the inclusivity and accessibility of educational settings. Exploring the challenges presented by physical obstacles we study look at accessible architectural approaches in context of South Asia

The aim of this webpage is to make the information behind accessible architecture design accessible to as many people as possible so that anyone interested can look it up and go through it at a glance.

The Impact of Physical Barriers in Schools

Image taken from unicef.org

Physical barriers in schools can significantly hinder the participation and learning of students with disabilities by limited access, isolation, safety concerns, stigmatization, etc.

  • Limited Access: Obstacles like stairs or narrow doorways can prevent students with mobility issues from entering certain parts of the school.
  • Isolation: Lack of access to common areas can leave students with disabilities feeling isolated and excluded, which impacts their social life.
  • Safety Concerns: Unsafe conditions, such as slippery floors or poor lighting, pose risks to students in general, especially students with disabilities.
  • Stigmatization: Physical barriers can also make students with disabilities feel marginalized as it highlights the difference from their peers and could lead to stigma.

All the implementation or lack of implementation we see are a result of economic factors, lack of awareness or lack of expertise. Some of the main reasons behind the lack of accessible architecture are:

  • Lack of Awareness: There is a general lack of awareness among architects, designers, and policymakers about the importance of disability-related design and the specific needs of individuals with disabilities. This lack of awareness leads to a neglect of inclusive design principles in architectural practices.
  • Limited Training: Architects and designers in South Asia often do not receive formal education or training on inclusive design practices for individuals with disabilities. Without proper training, professionals may not fully understand how to incorporate accessibility features into their designs.
  • Absence of Mandates: The absence of mandatory accessibility standards and guidelines for school design in India contributes to the lack of emphasis on disability-related design. Without clear regulations requiring inclusive design practices, architects may not prioritize accessibility features in their projects.
  • Cost Considerations: Implementing disability-related design features can sometimes be perceived as costly and may require additional resources. In a context where budget constraints are common, architects and builders may prioritize cost-efficiency over inclusive design.
  • Perception of Disability: Negative attitudes and misconceptions about disability in society can influence design decisions. Architects may not fully understand the diverse needs of individuals with disabilities or may underestimate the impact of physical barriers on their daily lives.

Objectives of This Webpage

Through this webpage, we will identify common architectural barriers in schools and discuss solutions and strategies to overcome them. This content is intended for educational purposes for anyone interested in learning more about accessible architecture.

Entry / Exit

In many cases in South Asia, the entry or exit to the school is muddy or there are some sorts of barriers. In some of these situations, there are stairs but no ramp to access. This causes issues for students with disabilities to access the building.

Image collected from unicef.org

The school needs to ensure there is a well-maintained, firm, and level surface for entry or exit. This makes sure students using a wheelchair can independently come in and go out of the school.

Ramp

Ramps are needed as a replacement for stairs. Since students need to access or go upstairs, in many scenarios there are either problems with the ramp being blocked or an unmaintained ramp.

Sometimes, ramps are designed without considering basic architecture or even physics principles. This often causes issues with using the ramp for the main purpose of what they were designed for at the first place

Signage

Signage is another element that is almost non-existent at South Asian schools. This causes difficulty for students to navigate, especially if it's a large school. As a result, it's important to consider installing signs at proper head clearance space around 1000mm high with contrasting colors for better visibiliy.

Image collected from unicef.com

Doors

Designing proper and wide enough doors is very important to ensure that the classroom or facility is accessible to everyone. From personal experience, I have noticed there to be bumps at the bottom of the doorway to keep rainwater out. This could potentially create complications for students accessing the classroom.

A proper design of a door looks like one with a D handle, kick plate, and being able to see through the door. Also, the addition of an automatic door opener could vastly improve accessibility issues.

Image collected from https://www.automaticdoorsauburn.com/

Classrooms

In many scenarios, there is often a lack of basic classroom equipment at the classroom which needs to be taken into consideration. In most cases, it's due to a lack of funding, but we still need to consider the necessary design.

Desks and Benches

Instead of just having uniform benches, there needs to be special benches for students who require that. However, it's not necessarily a financially difficult decision. As long as the authorities are willing to do that, it should be able to be done.

In order to make the desks more accessible, the designers could order benches that are higher or adjustable. And leave some row or some section with higher space.

Flooring

In many scenarios, I found very ill-maintained floors which I also got to observe during my days in Bangladesh. Such ill-maintenance can be very dangerous.

Playground

Playgrounds are very important places for socializing and recess, especially for children. In order to improve such places, a few very simple things are needed to be considered.

A playground can be made more accessible by adding a paved path from the school to the playground, adding ramps if there is a difference in the level. It also needs to be ensured that there is sufficient grass to lessen the chances of children getting hurt.

Toilets

Unmaintained toilets and toilets without the proper design make them inaccessible to students with locomotor disabilities.

In order to make toilets accessible, western toilets with installing handles the proper way needs to be done.
